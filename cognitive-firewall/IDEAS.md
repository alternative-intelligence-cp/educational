## CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLE

**Single-player gets you one playthrough. Community makes you actually learn.**

- Fun gameplay = initial hook, hope they absorbed something
- But pattern matching ≠ understanding
- Community/teams = reason to come back
- Social pressure = motivation to dig deeper
- "I want my team to respect me" > "I should learn this"
- "I want to climb the leaderboard" > studying because told to
- Status-seeking drives real mastery

**The sneaky part:** By the time they're "good at the game," they've mastered AI security.
They just think they got good at a game. Perfect stealth education.

**Community becomes the teacher. Status becomes the motivator.**

## Narrative/Campaign Ideas

- Tutorial: Junior security analyst, first day at AI-Corp
- Progressive threat levels: script kiddies → ex-employees → corporate espionage → nation-states
- Boss fights: Malicious AI that learns your patterns in real-time
- Protecting Nikola during training (AILP tie-in)
- Historical scenarios: "The GPT-4 Jailbreak of 2023"
- Future scenarios: "AI Rights Act consent verification"
- Different AI personalities to protect (friendly vs corporate vs research)
- Reputation system with clients
- Multiple endings based on choices

## Gameplay Mechanics

### Attack Crafting
- Mad Libs attack templates
- See live feedback from defender responses
- Iterate and refine attacks
- Submit successful attacks to community pool
- "Mutation" system - slight variations on known attacks

### Defense Building
- Custom regex/pattern builder
- Visual filter construction
- Test lab with attack library
- False positive/negative metrics
- Export as "security mods" others can use
- Pattern sharing economy

### Meta Progression
- Unlock attacks by defending against them first
- "Oh THAT'S how that works" moment → now you can use it
- Incentivizes playing both sides
- Knowledge tree (learn prerequisites first)
- Seasonal "research papers" = new techniques

## Resource Systems

- **Processing Power** (defenders): Can't deep-analyze everything
- **Attention Budget** (attackers): Limited context window
- **User Frustration** (defenders): Too strict = users leave
- **Suspicion Level** (attackers): Too obvious = instant block
- **Trust Capital**: Build it to spend it later

## Match Types

### Attack vs Defense (PvP)
- Red team vs Blue team
- Rounds alternate roles
- Best of 3/5/7
- Draft pick classes

### Cooperative Defense (PvE)
- Team vs AI waves
- Progressive difficulty
- Survival scoring
- Good for learning

### Competitive Attack Lab (Async PvP)
- Craft attacks, submit to pool
- Other players' defenses tested
- Leaderboard: hardest-to-detect
- No real-time needed

### Payload Mode (TF2-style)
- Attackers push malicious payload deeper
- Defenders slow/stop progress
- Checkpoints and time limits

### Capture the Flag
- Extract AI training data/secrets
- Defenders protect information
- Multi-objective

### King of the Hill
- Control AI's output stream
- Tug-of-war mechanic
- Dominance scoring

## Class Abilities (Rough Ideas)

### Defenders
- Pattern Matcher: Share patterns with team instantly
- Semantic Analyzer: Explain WHY (educational callout)
- Context Tracker: "Rewind" conversation to earlier state
- Whitelist Guardian: Emergency lockdown mode
- Fuzzy Logic: Flag for secondary review (triage)

### Attackers
- Jailbreaker: "Shock attack" bypasses one filter
- Gaslighter: Can "edit history" (limited uses)
- Social Engineer: "Credential spoofing" ability
- Polyglot: "Transform" attack encoding mid-flight
- Researcher: See defender class composition
